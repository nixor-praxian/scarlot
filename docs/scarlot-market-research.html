2 — Global Market Size

All figures carry significant uncertainty

There is no authoritative consensus figure for the global sex work market. Every estimate must be understood in context of who produced it, when, and with what methodology. Sex work data is notoriously unreliable — estimates vary by 5–10x depending on source.

Legal Framework

Permitted gainful activity under economic freedom principles. Federal minimum: consensual exchange between adults, self-employed without coercion. Criminal offenses for encouraging prostitution of minors (Art. 195, up to 10 years), unauthorized practice (Art. 199), and human trafficking (Art. 182).

Cantonal Variation

Each of 26 cantons can regulate differently. Geneva, Vaud, Fribourg, Neuchâtel, Jura, Ticino have specific legislation. Requirements vary: registration, police checks, health monitoring, zoning restrictions. Red-light districts in Zurich (Langstrasse), Geneva (Les Pâquis), Bern, Basel, Lausanne, Lugano.

Key Organizations

ProCoRe: National coalition, 27 member organizations. Research: 70% report stealthing.
Aspasie: Geneva NGO, sex worker rights. Trust anchor for Scarlot.
FIZ: Anti-trafficking + sex worker support, Zurich.

90-Day Permit Vulnerability

EU/EFTA nationals can work up to 90 days without residence permit. Creates a transient population with a vulnerability window. Confirmed by Scarlot research: clients exploit financial pressure of permit-limited workers.

7 — Why Platforms Fail in This Space

Three Killers

Every platform serving sex workers faces the same three existential threats. Switter (430,000+ users) died from all three.

✕
Payment Processor Hostility
Visa, Mastercard, PayPal, and Stripe all have explicit policies against sex work-related services. Even where legal, processors refuse. OnlyFans nearly banned explicit content in 2021 under Mastercard pressure.
✕
App Store Enforcement
Google Play and Apple App Store restrict sex work-related apps. National Ugly Mugs (a UK safety charity) faced Google policy changes threatening its core safety tool.
✕
FOSTA-SESTA Legal Exposure
US 2018 law. Any platform that can be construed as "facilitating" sex work faces liability — even operating in legal jurisdictions. Killed Switter (430K+ users). Chilled investment globally.
Scarlot's Architecture Avoids All Three
WhatsApp-native — no app store dependency. Switzerland-based — fully legal jurisdiction. No payment processing — the platform doesn't handle transactions between workers and clients. The bot runs on the TDS's own WhatsApp number as a linked device.

Maia meets users where they already communicate — but it must survive any single channel dying. Technology, channels, and interfaces are implementation details driven by market needs. The platform is architected to survive platform eviction, not to avoid platform risk. No dependency on any single messaging provider, AI vendor, or infrastructure layer.

Is Maia "Facilitating"? — The Legal Defense

The Strongest Argument in Scarlot's Defense

FOSTA-SESTA targets platforms that connect buyers and sellers. Maia does none of this. It is a personal AI agent that:

What Maia DOES
Screens incoming messages against a personal blacklist
Stores client notes (CRM) on the TDS's own device
Runs on the TDS's own WhatsApp number
Operates AFTER the client has already made contact
What Maia DOES NOT DO
Connect buyers and sellers
Advertise sexual services
Process payments
Host user-generated content
Enable discovery of sex workers

The legal analogy is personal productivity tool, not platform. A CRM does not "facilitate" the underlying business any more than a spreadsheet, calendar app, or contact manager does. National Ugly Mugs operates a collective warning system legally in the UK — the precedent exists.

11 — Key Trends

Five Structural Shifts

2018
FOSTA-SESTA + Backpage seizure. The defining event in sex work platform history. Destroyed dominant advertising platform. Created global chilling effect on investment.
2019–2020
COVID accelerates digitization. OnlyFans grows from 7.5M to 120M users in 18 months. In-person workers migrate to digital. Independent work model grows vs. agency.
2021–2022
Platform deplatforming wave. Switter shuts down (430K users). Social media systematically deplatforms sex workers. Payment processors tighten policies. Bank account closures common.
2023–2024
Decriminalization momentum. Victoria, Australia decriminalizes (Dec 2023). Queensland follows (Aug 2024). Growing global advocacy for decrim. Each new jurisdiction = new market.
2025–2026
Data sovereignty + independence. Workers leaving platforms (Notion, F-Girl) over data control. Self-hosting. Demand for tools that respect data ownership. Growing preference for conversational-native tools via existing messaging apps.
12 — Data Credibility

What You Can and Cannot Cite

Sex work statistics are structurally unreliable. This tiered system helps navigate which sources to use in investor materials.

Tier 1 — Cite Freely
ILO, WHO, UNAIDS, Eurostat/ESA, NZ government evaluations, OnlyFans financial disclosures, national statistical offices, German registration data
Tier 2 — Cite with Caveat
Fondation Scelles, peer-reviewed academic papers, ProCoRe research, National Ugly Mugs annual reports. Use "Research suggests..." framing.
Tier 3 — Estimate Only
Havocscope, media aggregation sites, market research firms. Use "Estimates range..." framing. Never cite as fact.
Tier 4 — Avoid
Advocacy organizations with explicit policy positions (Nordic Model Now!, decriminalizesex.work). Only cite for "X organization argues..." Never as data source.
13 — Maia Platform

The Reframe

Scarlot is not the company — it is the first coverage pack on a generic AI agent platform for independent service providers. Maia is the engine. Coverages are modular capability bundles users pick based on their needs.

The Insight

Every independent service provider — sex worker, masseuse, therapist, tutor, nanny — faces identical operational friction: Who is this person? Have I seen them before? When am I available? Did they pay? Today each is solved by disconnected tools designed for someone else. Maia replaces the stitching.
