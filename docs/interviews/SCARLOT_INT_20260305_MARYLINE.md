## 13. Researcher Notes & Bias Flags

**Bias flag 1: Survivorship bias in "mon système fonctionne"**
Maryline has been practicing for 13 years. Her system works because she has refined it over a decade. This does NOT mean the system would work for a TDS with 1-2 years of experience who has not yet built the tacit knowledge, emoji vocabulary, or boundary-enforcement habits. There is a risk of over-indexing on her confidence and under-weighting the needs of less experienced TDS who are Scarlot's more likely early adopters.

**Bias flag 2: Questionnaire format limits depth and spontaneity**
Written questionnaires produce more guarded, edited responses than live interviews. Maryline's answers are articulate but compact. A live interview might have revealed more nuance, especially around emotional dimensions (the death threat is described almost matter-of-factly). The absence of follow-up probing means several threads were left unpulled (WhatsApp usage, data portability concerns, WTP).

**Bias flag 3: Geographic mismatch - Paris vs. Switzerland**
This is the first non-Swiss participant. French law, French tools (Projet Jasmine), French market dynamics.
