## 13. Researcher Notes & Bias Flags

BIAS FLAG 13.1 - SURVIVORSHIP BIAS (CRITICAL)
  Meron's core narrative is "my system works because of my experience." This is textbook survivorship bias. She has survived and adapted over years, which means:
  (a) Her current system genuinely works for her, at the cost of 40% time overhead
  (b) Her experience cannot be transferred to newcomers through any tool
  (c) Her pain points may be structurally different from less experienced TDS
  (d) Her Q6 answer ("pas vraiment" - no real limits) should not be taken as evidence that the problems are solved; they are managed, at high cost, by an expert
  IMPACT ON RESEARCH: She validates that the problems exist (P3 - no inbound filtering, P4 - cognitive overload, P5 - no centralised blacklist, P6 - no-shows & time-wasters, P9 - no client KYC) but her workarounds are experience-dependent and not generalizable. Her "no limits" response could lead researchers to underweight these problems if taken at face value.

BIAS FLAG 13.2 - WRITTEN FORMAT SUPPRESSION
  Written questionnaires systematically suppress:
  - Emotional expression (no tone, no pauses, no body language)
  - Narrative depth (people write less than they say)
  - Follow-up opportunities (no probing on Q2 non-answer)
  - Specific examples (writing out a detailed incident is effortful)
  All of Meron's responses are one to two sentences. A live interview with this participant would likely yield 3-5x more material. The brevity should not be interpreted as shallow engagement - she responded thoughtfully, just within the constraints of the format.

BIAS FLAG 13.3 - CONFIRMATION RISK ON 40% FIGURE
  The 40% figure is the most compelling quantitative signal in the dataset so far. There is a risk of anchoring on this number and over-weighting it in decision-making. Important caveats:
  - Self-estimated, not measured
  - "Je dirais" (I would say) - hedging language
  - No definition of "unwanted messages" was provided - her threshold may differ from others'
  - The Q2 non-answer weakens the supporting evidence (no specific example to validate the estimate)

BIAS FLAG 13.4 - RESEARCHER PROJECTION RISK
  Meron's brief answers create large interpretive gaps that researchers may fill with their own assumptions. Every inference in this record (especially in sections 04, 06, 08) should be treated as hypothesis, not finding. The recommended next action (RA-M1) is designed to replace projection with data.

BIAS FLAG 13.5 - DISTRIBUTION CHANNEL BIAS
  The questionnaire was distributed via the co-founder's trusted network. Meron's willingness to respond may correlate with her existing trust relationship with the co-founder or network members. Her views may not represent TDS outside this trust circle.

NOTE ON EVIDENCE QUALITY:
  This interview produces exclusively Silver and Bronze signals. No Gold-tier evidence is possible from a written questionnaire format - Gold requires unprompted, specific, past-tense behavioral statements with emotional intensity, and the structured question format inherently makes all responses prompted.
